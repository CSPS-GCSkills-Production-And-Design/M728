Optimizing Energy Performance of Existing Buildings

To support Canada’s goal to reduce its greenhouse gas emissions by 80% by 2050, relative to 2005 levels, federal facilities have committed to take practical steps to reduce energy consumption and carbon emissions from their operations. This course supports their efforts to deliver on their commitments.

This course has been adapted from the US Department of Energy’s Federal Energy Management Program training material. This training is being delivered through the Natural Resources Canada Greening Government Services, to help federal departments and agencies plan and implement projects that reduce the energy use and greenhouse gas emissions in their buildings, laboratories, other facilities and vehicle fleets.

This course introduces the four guiding principles (metering, benchmarking, energy efficiency, renewable and clean energy) that should be followed sequentially on the journey to optimizing the energy performance of existing buildings.

This course will take approximately 30 minutes to complete. To successfully complete the course you must answer the 10-question exam at the end. There is no required pass mark.

This course is composed of 6 videos and includes a final exam, as follows:

  • Introduction
  • Guiding Principle 1 - Metering
  • Guiding Principle 2 - Benchmarking
  • Guiding Principle 3 - Energy Efficiency
  • Guiding Principle 4 - Renewable and Clean Energy
  • Summary
  • Exam
